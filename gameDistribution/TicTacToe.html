Tic Tac Toe

Tic Tac Toe looks simple at first glance, yet every turn is a small contest of timing, prediction, and control. Whether you are facing another player on the same board or testing yourself against the AI, the goal stays clear: place your mark with purpose and create a full line of three before your opponent can stop you.

Match Focus

Watch the center, protect open lines, and never ignore a block when the other side is one move away from winning.

How the game works

Two sides take turns placing X and O on the grid, one square at a time. You can play as a local two-player match or take on the AI for a quicker solo challenge. A round is won by completing three matching symbols in a horizontal row, vertical column, or diagonal line before the other side does.

What good play looks like

Strong Tic Tac Toe is about seeing two moves ahead. Claiming the center often gives the cleanest control, corners can create stronger pressure, and timely blocks are just as important as attacking lines of your own. Against the AI or another player, wins usually come from forcing choices rather than waiting for mistakes.
